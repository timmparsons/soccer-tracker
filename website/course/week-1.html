Week 1: Foundations — Days 1–7

30-Day Touch Challenge · 300–600 touches a day · 5–10 min/day

Week 1 teaches the five foundation moves every good touch is built on: toe taps, foundations, inside rolls, outside rolls, and sole rolls. Targets climb from 300 to 600 touches a day — about 3,150 touches for the week, never more than ten minutes a session.

Don’t rush this week. The moves feel basic because they are basic — they’re also the exact touches you’ll do thousands of times over the next 30 days. Slow and clean beats fast and sloppy for the first seven days. Speed comes in Week 2.

1

Toe Taps: Learn Your Baseline

Target: 300 touches

Goal: get comfortable with the ball under your feet and find out where you’re starting from.

Today’s routine

  1. Toe taps — 2 minutes, alternating feet tapping the top of the ball (~150 touches)
  2. Toe taps, small hops — 1 minute, add a light bounce between taps (~75 touches)
  3. Free touches — 1 minute, just move the ball around however feels natural (~75 touches)

Form cues

  • Stay on the balls of your feet — never flat-footed.
  • Touch the top of the ball lightly; don’t stomp it.
  • Keep your eyes up for a few seconds at a time. It feels weird now. It pays off forever.

Bonus: count how many juggles you can do in a row and write the number down. That’s your Day 1 baseline — you’ll test it again on Day 7, 14, 21, and 29.

2

Foundations (Bell Touches)

Target: 350 touches

Goal: learn the single most-used training touch in soccer — the ball ping-ponging between your insteps.

Today’s routine

  1. Foundations — 2 minutes, tap the ball side to side between the insides of both feet (~200 touches)
  2. Foundations, moving forward — 1 minute, same touch while inching up the yard (~75 touches)
  3. Toe taps — 1 minute to revisit Day 1 (~75 touches)

Form cues

  • Knees bent, weight low — like sitting slightly into a chair.
  • Keep the ball on the line between your feet; if it drifts, slow down.
  • Quiet touches. If someone can hear the ball smacking, you’re hitting too hard.
3

Inside Rolls

Target: 400 touches

Goal: start moving the ball with the sole and inside of your foot — the touch you’ll use to escape defenders.

Today’s routine

  1. Inside rolls, right foot — 90 seconds, roll the ball across your body with the sole, stop it with the inside (~125 touches)
  2. Inside rolls, left foot — 90 seconds (~125 touches)
  3. Foundations — 90 seconds (~150 touches)

Form cues

  • Roll the ball with the middle of your sole, not your toes.
  • Your plant foot should hop as the ball moves — stay light.
  • Left foot gets equal time. No exceptions, all 30 days.

Three days in — make it official

Log each day’s touches in Master Touch and set your daily goal to the day’s target. The progress ring fills as you train, and your streak starts counting. Players who track their touches are the ones still here on Day 30.

4

Outside Touches & Rolls

Target: 450 touches

Goal: wake up the outside of your foot — the surface most beginners never train and most great dribblers live on.

Today’s routine

  1. Outside touches — 2 minutes, push the ball away and catch it with the outside of the same foot, both feet (~175 touches)
  2. Inside-outside — 90 seconds, one touch inside, one touch outside, same foot, then switch (~150 touches)
  3. Inside rolls — 90 seconds to revisit Day 3 (~125 touches)

Form cues

  • Point your toes slightly down and in for outside touches.
  • Small pushes — the ball should never get more than a step away.
  • If inside-outside feels clumsy, slow to half speed until it doesn’t.

Level up: comfortable already? Try inside-outside at jogging pace in a straight line — a taste of the intermediate drills in the Master Touch drill library.

5

Sole Rolls & Pull-Backs

Target: 500 touches

Goal: master the sole of the foot — the touch that stops the ball dead and changes direction in tight spaces.

Today’s routine

  1. Sole rolls sideways — 90 seconds, drag the ball across your body with the sole, both directions (~125 touches)
  2. Pull-backs — 90 seconds, pull the ball back under you and turn with it, alternating feet (~125 touches)
  3. Foundations — 90 seconds (~150 touches)
  4. Toe taps — 1 minute (~100 touches)

Form cues

  • On pull-backs, turn your whole body with the ball — not just your leg.
  • Keep your head over the ball as you drag it.
  • These work on any surface: garage, hallway, driveway. No excuses on rainy days.
6

The Five-Move Circuit

Target: 550 touches

Goal: chain all five foundation moves into one continuous circuit for the first time.

Today’s routine

  1. Toe taps — 1 minute (~100 touches)
  2. Foundations — 1 minute (~100 touches)
  3. Inside rolls — 1 minute, both feet (~100 touches)
  4. Outside touches — 1 minute, both feet (~100 touches)
  5. Sole rolls & pull-backs — 90 seconds (~150 touches)

Form cues

  • Move from drill to drill without stopping — the transition is part of the training.
  • Quality first: if a move falls apart, drop the speed, not the move.
  • This circuit is the skeleton of everything in Weeks 2–4. Learn to love it.
7

Test Day

Target: 600 touches

Goal: your first full test — 600 touches in one session, plus a juggling check-in.

Today’s routine

  1. The five-move circuit — run Day 6’s full circuit (~550 touches)
  2. Free dribbling — 30–60 seconds of cuts and turns to finish (~50 touches)
  3. Juggling test — three attempts at your best run. Compare with Day 1.

Form cues

  • Don’t pace yourself into slow motion — train at the speed you’d play.
  • Write down (or log) your juggle record. Most players beat their Day 1 number already.

Week 1 complete: that’s roughly 3,150 touches — more deliberate ball work than many players get in a month of team practice. Week 2 turns up the speed.
